Drawing techniques



Here are some drawing techniques that you can try:



Sketching:




This is the process of making quick and rough drawings to capture the basic shapes, forms, and proportions of your subject. Sketching is useful for developing your observation skills and exploring different ideas before making a final drawing.



Shading:




This is the technique of creating different values of light and dark on your drawing to create the illusion of depth and volume. You can use different tools and methods to create shading, such as pencils, charcoal, hatching, cross-hatching, blending, etc.



Perspective:




This is the technique of creating the illusion of distance and space on a flat surface. Perspective helps you draw realistic scenes and objects that appear to recede or come closer to the viewer. You can use different types of perspective, such as one-point, two-point, three-point, etc.



Contour drawing:




This is the technique of drawing the outline or edge of an object or shape without any shading or details. Contour drawing helps you focus on the form and structure of your subject and improve your hand-eye coordination. You can use different types of contour drawing, such as blind contour, cross-contour, continuous line, etc.



Gesture drawing:




This is the technique of drawing the movement and energy of a living figure or object in a short amount of time. Gesture drawing helps you capture the essence and emotion of your subject and develop your sense of proportion and rhythm. You can use gesture drawing to practice drawing human figures, animals, plants, etc.
